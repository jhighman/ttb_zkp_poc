Imagine being able to verify that someone is a licensed doctor, has a clean criminal record, or meets a company's hiring criteria‚Äîwithout ever seeing the underlying documents or personal identifiers. That's the power of zero-knowledge proofs.

ZKPs let one party (the prover) convince another (the verifier) that a statement is true, without revealing why it's true. The cryptographic magic relies on three principles:
