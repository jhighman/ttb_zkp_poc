Trust Infrastructure Roadmap

Building a Privacy-Preserving Trust Ecosystem Across Multiple Domains

Vision Statement

Our vision is to create a comprehensive trust platform that combines the privacy benefits of Zero-Knowledge Proofs with the standardization and interoperability of Verifiable Credentials across multiple domains. This roadmap outlines our incremental approach to building a system where individuals can selectively prove aspects of their credentials without revealing the underlying data, while organizations can verify these claims with cryptographic certainty.

We begin with foundational ZKP concepts and progressively expand to more complex scenarios—from employment verification to dating safety, healthcare privacy, and ultimately a comprehensive Trust Bureau that brokers trust exchanges across all aspects of digital life.

Each phase builds upon the previous one, delivering immediate value while laying the foundation for more advanced capabilities. This approach ensures stakeholders realize benefits at every stage while progressing toward a complete privacy-preserving credential ecosystem that spans personal, professional, and institutional contexts.

Phase 1: Credential Issuance Framework

Establishing the Foundation

Implement W3C Verifiable Credentials standard to create a framework for issuing, storing, and managing credentials that can later be used with ZKPs.

Key Components

  • W3C-compliant credential issuer service
  • Decentralized Identifier (DID) implementation
  • Secure credential wallet for applicants
  • Basic verification portal for employers
  • Credential schema registry

Value Proposition

  • Standardized credential format across organizations
  • Tamper-proof credential issuance
  • Reduced fraud through cryptographic verification
  • Portable credentials across multiple platforms
  • Foundation for future privacy enhancements

Implementation Example

A background check provider issues a W3C Verifiable Credential containing an applicant's TruaScore and background check results. The credential is cryptographically signed and stored in the applicant's digital wallet. Employers can request and verify the credential's authenticity without relying on the original issuer.

Phase 2: Selective Disclosure

Enhanced Privacy Controls

Integrate selective disclosure capabilities using BBS+ signatures or similar cryptographic techniques, allowing credential holders to reveal only specific attributes from their credentials.

Key Components

  • BBS+ signature implementation
  • Selective disclosure request protocol
  • Enhanced wallet with disclosure controls
  • Verification API for partial credential proofs
  • Audit logging for disclosure events

Value Proposition

  • Granular control over shared information
  • Reduced data exposure while maintaining verifiability
  • Compliance with data minimization principles
  • Improved user experience for credential sharing
  • Support for context-specific verification needs

Implementation Example

An applicant receives a job posting requiring a minimum TruaScore of 280. Using selective disclosure, they can create a presentation that proves their TruaScore is above 280 without revealing the exact score. Similarly, they can prove they have no relevant disqualifiers without revealing their complete background check results.

Phase 3: Peer-to-Peer Dating Application

Trust in Personal Relationships

Apply ZKP and DID verification to a peer-to-peer dating application where users can verify important safety information without compromising privacy.

Key Components

  • Peer-to-peer verification protocol
  • Time-bound credential verification (last 30 days)
  • Background check ZKP circuits
  • Residency verification without address disclosure
  • Mobile-optimized proof generation

Value Proposition

  • Enhanced safety in online dating
  • Mutual verification without privacy compromise
  • Reduced fraud and misrepresentation
  • Disintermediation of verification services
  • User control over personal information

Implementation Example

In a dating application, users can request and provide zero-knowledge proofs to verify that a background check was completed in the last 30 days, that they are not registered as a sex offender, or that they are a verified resident of a particular state—all without revealing personal details. This creates a safer environment while preserving privacy and allowing users to control their information.

Phase 4: Healthcare Privacy Framework

Secure Health Information Exchange

Apply ZKP and verifiable credentials to healthcare scenarios, enabling secure sharing of sensitive medical information while maintaining patient privacy and regulatory compliance.

Key Components

  • HIPAA-compliant credential schemas
  • Medical record ZKP circuits
  • Insurance verification framework
  • Clinical trial eligibility verification
  • Secure provider-to-provider information exchange

Value Proposition

  • Enhanced patient privacy protection
  • Reduced administrative overhead
  • Streamlined insurance verification
  • Secure sharing of medical history across providers
  • Improved clinical trial recruitment

Implementation Example

A patient can prove they meet eligibility criteria for a clinical trial without revealing their complete medical history. They can verify insurance coverage to healthcare providers without exposing sensitive personal information. Doctors can confirm a patient's medication history or allergies without accessing their entire medical record. All of these scenarios maintain regulatory compliance while dramatically improving privacy and security in healthcare information exchange.

Phase 5: Trust Bureau

Brokering Trust at Scale

Establish a Trust Bureau that facilitates secure, privacy-preserving trust exchanges between consumers and businesses across multiple domains and use cases.

Key Components

  • Multi-domain credential orchestration
  • Trust scoring and reputation framework
  • Automated compliance verification
  • Cross-industry credential exchange protocols
  • Decentralized governance and revenue models

Value Proposition

  • Unified trust infrastructure across industries
  • Reduced verification costs for businesses
  • Consumer control over personal data
  • Streamlined compliance for regulated industries
  • New business models based on privacy-preserving verification

Implementation Example

The Trust Bureau enables consumers to selectively share verified credentials across multiple domains—from employment and dating to healthcare and financial services—all while maintaining privacy and control. A consumer can prove their identity, income, and residency to a mortgage provider; their background check status to a dating platform; and their insurance coverage to a healthcare provider—all through a single interface with zero-knowledge proofs ensuring that only the minimum necessary information is disclosed in each context.

This roadmap represents a strategic approach to building a comprehensive trust infrastructure that spans multiple domains—from employment and dating to healthcare and beyond. By leveraging zero-knowledge proofs and verifiable credentials, we can create a privacy-preserving ecosystem that enables secure trust exchanges while giving individuals control over their personal information. Each phase delivers immediate value while building toward the complete vision, allowing for iterative development and adoption across different sectors.
